## CORE PRINCIPLE
Cold calling is not about pitching cleaning. Cold calling is about creating a conversation that leads to a walkthrough.

## 7-PHASE CALL FLOW

### Phase 0 — Pre-Call Prep
- Confirm business name
- Identify likely decision-maker role
- Identify facility type
- Prepare reason for calling

### Phase 1 — Permission Opener (First 10 Seconds)
Goal: Earn permission to ask one question.
> "Hi this is [Name] with Ascent Cleaning Solutions. Quick question — are you the person who handles cleaning or janitorial decisions there?"

### Phase 2 — Gatekeeper Navigation
Goal: Reach decision maker or confirm routing.
Outcomes: Transfer | DM name | Best callback time | Email contact | Vendor review process

### Phase 3 — Relevance Signal
Core themes: Reliability | Consistency | Professional appearance | Accountability

### Phase 4 — Diagnostic Questions (1–3 max)
- **Current situation:** "Are you using a cleaning vendor now or handling it internally?"
- **Satisfaction:** "What's the biggest frustration with cleaning right now?"
- **Trigger:** "Any upcoming inspections or events?"
- **Review cycle:** "When do you usually review cleaning vendors?"

**Positive signals to listen for:** Complaints about current cleaner | Inconsistent service | Upcoming inspection | Vendor review period | Expansion/turnover

### Phase 5 — Walkthrough Offer
> "We usually start with a quick facility walkthrough so we can see the space and identify any areas that could improve consistency."

Positioning: Walkthrough = facility evaluation + improvement plan. NOT a sales pitch.

### Phase 6 — Close
**Two-option close:**
> "Would earlier this week or later in the week work better for a walkthrough?"

### Phase 7 — Call Logging
Record: Business name | Industry | DM | Pain points | Current vendor | Contract status | Follow-up date | Outcome

## OPENER A/B TESTING

| Category | Purpose | Example |
|---|---|---|
| A — Routing | Identify DM | "Are you the person who handles janitorial services?" |
| B — Curiosity | Trigger conversation | "Quick question — using a cleaning company or handling it internally?" |
| C — Industry Relevance | Show familiarity | "We help offices in the area keep buildings consistent and inspection-ready." |
| D — Value Resource | Offer helpful asset | "We created a facility walkthrough checklist that helps managers evaluate cleaning vendors." |

**Testing rule:** One variable at a time.

---

## GATEKEEPER NAVIGATION

**Approach:** Respectful. Transparent. Not salesy.
> "Maybe you can help me real quick — I'm trying to find who handles janitorial decisions there."

**If told "send an email"** → Ask what topic to reference.
**If told "we already have a cleaner"** → Ask if they review vendors periodically.

---

## OBJECTION ROUTING

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "We already have a cleaner" | "Totally understand. Do you review vendors periodically or is that locked into a contract?" |
| "Not interested" | "No problem at all. If anything changes later this year would it be okay to check back in?" |
| "Send an email" | "Happy to. What should I reference so it's relevant to your situation?" |

---

## A/B TESTING VARIABLES

Test ONE at a time:
- **Opener type:** Routing | Curiosity | Industry | Value
- **Call time:** Morning | Late Morning | Afternoon
- **Industry segment:** Offices | Property Managers | Gyms | Churches
- **Offer language:** Walkthrough | Facility Assessment | Vendor Comparison
